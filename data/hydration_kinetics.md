# Hydration Kinetics and Electrolyte Homeostasis
**Category:** Hydration
**Year:** 2024

Hydration is a state of 'Euhydration,' regulated by the kidneys and the antidiuretic hormone (ADH/Vasopressin). The human body maintains Plasma Osmolality between 280 and 295 mOsm/kg. When osmolality rises, the hypothalamus triggers the thirst mechanism. The 'WUT' criteria (Weight, Urine color, Thirst) is the simplest tool for monitoring hydration. A loss of >2% body mass indicates clinically significant dehydration.

Hyponatremia (plasma sodium <135 mmol/L) is a life-threatening condition caused by 'over-hydrating' with plain water during prolonged exercise, diluting the extracellular sodium. This can lead to cerebral edema. Electrolytes, particularly Sodium (extracellular) and Potassium (intracellular), maintain the electrochemical gradient required for nerve impulse transmission and muscle contraction via the Na+/K+-ATPase pump.

The 2024 guidelines emphasize that 'thirst is a late indicator.' For athletes, sweat testing (measuring mg of sodium lost per liter of sweat) allows for personalized hydration plans. The Adequate Intake (AI) remains 3.7L for men and 2.7L for women, but up to 20% of this should come from water-rich foods like cucumbers and melons.
