# Menstrual Cycle Syncing: Phase-Specific Nutrition
**Focus:** Hormonal Optimization for Women (Age 25)
**Keywords:** Menstrual, Follicular, Ovulatory, Luteal, Estrogen, Progesterone

Hormonal fluctuations during the 28-day menstrual cycle significantly alter metabolic rate, insulin sensitivity, and nutrient requirements.

1. **Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5):** Progesterone and estrogen are at their lowest. The primary physiological stress is iron loss and uterine inflammation. **Recommended Foods:** Focus on iron-rich sources (Heme iron from grass-fed beef or Non-heme from lentils with Vitamin C) to replenish blood stores. Anti-inflammatory foods like ginger, turmeric, and dark chocolate (>70% cacao) help mitigate prostaglandin-induced cramping.

2. **Follicular Phase (Days 6-13):** Estrogen begins to rise, increasing insulin sensitivity and energy levels. The body is more efficient at utilizing carbohydrates for fuel. **Recommended Foods:** Support estrogen metabolism with cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower) containing Indole-3-Carbinol. Fermented foods like kimchi or kefir support the 'estrobolome'—gut bacteria that help metabolize and eliminate hormones.

3. **Ovulatory Phase (Day 14):** Estrogen peaks, often leading to a surge in energy and a slight rise in body temperature. **Recommended Foods:** High-fiber foods (quinoa, berries, flaxseeds) are critical here to ensure excess estrogen is bound and excreted through the digestive tract, preventing 'estrogen dominance.' Anti-inflammatory Omega-3s from wild-caught salmon or algae oil support the follicle's release.

4. **Luteal Phase (Days 15-28):** Progesterone dominates, raising the basal metabolic rate (BMR) by 100-300 calories. Insulin sensitivity drops, making high-sugar foods more likely to cause fat storage and energy crashes. **Recommended Foods:** Focus on Magnesium-rich foods (pumpkin seeds, spinach) to reduce PMS-related water retention and anxiety. Complex carbohydrates (sweet potatoes, chickpeas) provide the glucose necessary for serotonin production, preventing the common 'Luteal Binge' on refined sugars.
