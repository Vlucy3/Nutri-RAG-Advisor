# Lifecycle Nutrition: From Gestation to Geriatrics
**Category:** Lifecycle
**Year:** 2024

Nutritional requirements are dynamic across the lifespan. In the Gestation phase, Folate (400–600 mcg) is critical during the first 28 days of pregnancy to prevent neural tube defects (NTDs) like spina bifida. The 'First 1000 Days' (conception to age 2) is a critical window for epigenetic programming; choline and iron are essential for neurodevelopment.

In Pediatrics, the focus is on growth and the prevention of 'picky eating' through early exposure to diverse flavors. In Adolescence, Calcium (1,300 mg) and Phosphorus are prioritized as 90% of peak bone mass is achieved by age 18.

In the Geriatric population (65+), the primary concern is Sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss). To combat this, the 2024 consensus suggests increasing protein intake to 1.2–1.5 g/kg of body mass (higher than the standard 0.8g/kg). Additionally, the production of Intrinsic Factor and gastric acid decreases with age, making Vitamin B12 absorption from food difficult. Therefore, B12-fortified foods or supplements are recommended for older adults. Bone health also remains a priority, with Calcium requirements increasing to 1,200 mg for women over 50.
